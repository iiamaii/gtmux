01 · Caption

Caption

Pinned annotation block — used to tag a shape, sketch, or region with a figure number and a short editorial note. Accent rail on the left ties it visually to whatever it annotates.

type: caption
Light

Early exploration of the onboarding flow — letting users touch the canvas before any auth gating.
Dark
Fig. 02 · Ideation sketch 2m · JK
Early exploration of the onboarding flow — letting users touch the canvas before any auth gating.
Anatomy
  • 1
    Accent rail — 2px left border in --accent. Replaces a full border so the card reads as attached, not floating.
  • 2
    Cap-head — mono uppercase, 9.5px / 0.6px tracking. Pattern: ● Fig. NN · Topic author/time.
  • 3
    Dot — 6px accent dot, signals "this is annotation, not content".
  • 4
    Bold label — figure number / short noun. --fg / weight 540.
  • 5
    Trailing meta — pushed right with margin-left:auto. Author initials, edit time, or both.
  • 6
    Cap-body — sans, 12px / 1.4. One paragraph. If you need two — switch to a Document.

    Panel body — terminal background #0d0e10, foreground #e6e6e6. Theme-fixed — dark even when the page is light. Mono 11.5px / 1.55. Padding 12 14 14.

05 · Shared rules

Shared rules

Apply to all three components — they sit on the same canvas and need to read as a family.

cross-cut
Behaviour
  • A
    Outer frame — all three sit inside .canvas-item (absolute on the workspace). Width / height are pinned in style; the shape fills 100%.
  • B
    Selection — when selected, the canvas adds a 1.5px --accent outline around .canvas-item, not on the shape. Shapes don't manage selection state.
  • C
    Hover — no per-shape hover effect. Hover state is on the canvas item wrapper (slight outline). Keeps content quiet.
  • D
    Theme — every token is theme-reactive. Don't hard-code colours in shape styles except language badges and the terminal body (which is intentionally dark on both themes).
  • E
    Layer tree — shows the type + name. Caption icon: speech-bubble. Document: doc. Filepath: doc with </> mark.
  • F
    Overflow — all three use overflow: hidden. Long copy is the author's problem, not the shape's.
